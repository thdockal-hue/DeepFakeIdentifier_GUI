How it works

Two-layer analysis
Your image is first processed by 10 client-side algorithms running entirely in your browser — no data leaves your device. Then, optionally, Claude AI Vision examines the image for subtle visual artifacts that algorithms may miss.

Client-side Forensic Methods

🔬

Error Level Analysis

Re-saves at JPEG quality 75 and amplifies the difference. AI images often show abnormally flat or spiky ELA patterns.

📡

Noise Analysis

Extracts luminance noise via 3×3 residuals. GAN outputs are unnaturally smooth with near-zero noise floor.

📊

Frequency Analysis

Autocorrelation at lags 8/16/32. Convolutional upsampling in GANs leaves periodic spatial artifacts.

🎨

Color Coherence

Hue entropy + saturation variance. Diffusion models produce suspiciously narrow, uniform color distributions.

✂️

Edge Integrity

Sobel edge density coefficient of variation. Face-swap masks often flatten local sharpness variation unnaturally.

🏷️

Metadata Presence

Scans binary headers for EXIF/XMP. Real photos carry device metadata; AI images almost never do.

🧵

Texture Complexity

Local Binary Pattern entropy across 8×8 blocks. AI images often show homogeneous, low-entropy texture fields.

🟦

DCT Block Artifacts

Measures 8×8 block boundary discontinuities. AI generation and face-swap introduce distinctive blocking patterns.

📈

Channel Statistics

RGB skewness and kurtosis. Real photos follow natural statistical distributions; synthetic images often deviate.

⚖️

Symmetry Analysis

Bilateral pixel symmetry deviation. GAN-generated faces often show unnatural left-right symmetry patterns.
